Parental Substance Abuse

Effects on Children, Relationships, and Family Stability

Overview

This website explores how parental substance abuse affects children’s emotional development, behaviour, relationships, and family stability within Ontario.

Impact on Children

  • Anxiety, depression, emotional instability
  • Trust and attachment issues
  • Behavioural challenges at school
  • Parentification (taking on adult roles)

Intimate Relationships

  • Difficulty trusting partners
  • Fear of abandonment
  • Emotional withdrawal or dependence

Family Impact

  • Breakdown in communication
  • Role reversal between parent and child
  • Instability in routines and caregiving

Social Theory (Conflict Theory)

Conflict theory explains substance abuse as connected to inequality, stress, and limited access to resources. These social pressures increase vulnerability and affect entire family systems.

Norms, Roles & Institutions

Society expects parents to provide stability and care. When substance abuse disrupts this, schools, healthcare systems, and social services often intervene to support families.

Support Systems in Ontario

  • Addiction counselling services
  • Family therapy programs
  • Youth support services
  • Mental health organizations
