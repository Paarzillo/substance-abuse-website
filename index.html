Parental Substance Abuse

Understanding the impact on children, relationships, and family stability in Ontario

Overview

About This Issue

This resource explores how parental substance abuse affects emotional development, behaviour, relationships, and family stability.

Children

Impact on Children

  • Anxiety, depression, emotional instability
  • Difficulty forming secure attachments and trust
  • Behavioural challenges in school environments
  • Parentification and early responsibility
Relationships

Intimate Relationships

  • Trust and abandonment issues
  • Emotional withdrawal or dependency patterns
  • Difficulty forming healthy bonds
Family System

Family Impact

  • Breakdown in communication
  • Role reversal between parent and child
  • Instability in caregiving and routines
Theory

Conflict Theory

Substance abuse is linked to inequality, stress, and limited access to resources, affecting entire family systems.

Systems

Norms, Roles & Institutions

Schools, healthcare, and social services support families when parental roles and norms are disrupted.

Support

Support Systems in Ontario

  • Addiction counselling services
  • Family therapy programs
  • Youth mental health supports
  • Community organizations
